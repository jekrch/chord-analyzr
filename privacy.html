Privacy Policy

Modal Chord Buildr · Last updated: July 12, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Modal Chord Buildr ("the App", "we", "us") handles information when you use the application at https://modal.chordbuildr.com. Modal Chord Buildr is a free tool for building, arranging, and exploring musical chords and song sheets.

The App runs entirely in your web browser. We do not operate user accounts, and we do not sell your data or use it for advertising.

Information We Access and Store

Google Drive data

If you choose to connect Google Drive, the App uses the https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.file scope. This scope grants access only to files that the App itself creates or that you explicitly open with the App — it does not allow the App to see, list, or access any other files in your Google Drive.

Specifically, the App uses this access to:

  • Create and update a single file named chordbuildr-songs.json in your Google Drive, which stores your song library so you can sync it across devices.
  • Read that file back so your songs are available when you sign in again.

Authentication is handled by Google Identity Services. The App receives a short-lived (approximately one hour) access token that is held only in your browser's memory for the duration of your session. This token is never written to disk, never persisted, and never transmitted to us or any third party other than Google's own APIs. We do not receive or store your Google password, email address, or profile information.

Data stored on your device

Your songs, chord arrangements, and application settings are saved locally in your browser's storage (localStorage) on your own device. This data stays on your device and is not transmitted to us. You can clear it at any time through your browser's settings.

Music theory data

The App loads read-only music theory reference data (scales, modes, and chord definitions) from our servers. These requests contain no personal information and are not used to identify or track you.

Analytics

We use a privacy-focused, self-hosted analytics service (Umami, hosted on our own infrastructure) to understand aggregate usage, such as page views and general visitor counts. This service does not use cookies and does not collect personally identifiable information. Analytics data is aggregated and cannot be used to identify you individually.

How We Use Google User Data

Data accessed through Google Drive is used solely to provide the App's core feature of saving and syncing your song library, as described above. We do not use Google user data for any other purpose.

Limited Use disclosure. Modal Chord Buildr's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We do not transfer, sell, or use this data for serving advertisements, and we do not allow humans to read this data unless we have your affirmative consent, it is necessary for security purposes (such as investigating abuse), to comply with applicable law, or the data has been aggregated and anonymized.

Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties. Data stored in your Google Drive is stored within your own Google account under Google's terms and privacy policy. We do not transmit your song library or Google data to any server we control.

Data Retention and Deletion

  • On your device: clear your browser's site data / localStorage for this site to remove locally stored songs and settings.
  • In Google Drive: you can delete the chordbuildr-songs.json file directly from your Google Drive at any time.
  • Access revocation: you can revoke the App's Google Drive access at any time from your Google Account permissions page.

Security

All communication with Google APIs and our servers occurs over encrypted HTTPS connections. Because the App is client-side and access tokens are short-lived and held only in memory, we minimize the surface on which your data could be exposed.

Children's Privacy

The App is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.
